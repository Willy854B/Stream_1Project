About us

"The Monkees" has been a fixture on rock and pop music for well over five decades. It has evolved from its early days as a tv show rock band into one of the most successful music groups in the US and around the world. We were formed in Los Angeles in 1965 by Bob Rafelson and Bert Schneider for the American television series The Monkees which aired from 1966 to 1968. Screen Gems executive producer Don Kirshner came up with the idea of creating the tv show, which marked the beginning of the band in its early days until now. Since early 1960's, our musical acting quartet have sold more than 50 million records worldwide and had international hits, including "Last Train to Clarkville", "Pleasant Valley Sanday", "Day Dream Beleaver", and "I'm a Beleaver".

Micky Dolenz

Singer, musician, actor, writer, producer, director. Born on March 8, 1945 in Los Angeles, California. He had a few television roles and played with bands before being cast in The Monkees. Dolenz grew up in Los Angeles as the son of actor George Dolenz, who is best known for playing the Count of Monte Cristo on television. Mickey Dolenz launched his own acting career early on, taking the stage name Micky Braddock. From 1956 to 1958, he starred in the adventure series Circus Boy, playing an orphan adopted by a traveling circus. After the show was canceled, Dolenz landed a few guest appearances on such programs as Peyton Place. After high school, he studied architecture at Valley College and the Los Angeles Technical Institute. Davy Jones

English singer-songwriter, musician, actor and businessman. born in December 30, 1945 in Manchester, England. His acting credits include a Tony-nominated role as the Artful Dodger in the original London and Broadway productions of Oliver! as well as a starring cameo role in a hallmark episode of The Brady Bunch television show and later reprised parody film; Love, American Style; and My Two Dads. Basil Foster. Jones quit school in 1961 to train as an apprentice jockey, but the jockey Jones apprenticed with, recognized Jones' acting and singing ability, and encouraged him to pursue his acting career. His persistence paid off, and Jones had television parts in Coronation Street and June Evening, and an appearance on The Ed Sullivan Show that led Colpix Records/Columbia Pictures to sign a contract with Jones. Thanks to his English accent and smooth vocals, Jones was considered one of the great teen idols.

Michael Neshmith

American musician, songwriter, actor, producer, novelist, businessman, and philanthropist. Born December 30, 1942 in Houston, Texas. Nesmith attended Thomas Jefferson High School, where he performed in musicals and in the school's choir during his high school years, and later served in the U.S. Air Force before pursuing a career in music. After that, he went to San Antonio College. He developed a passion for folk music and started performing and recording his own compositions. In the mid-1960s, Nesmith moved to Los Angeles to advance his career. In 1965, Michael won a role on The Monkees where he became known as one of members of the pop rock band where he and the other band members began to take more creative control with 1967's Headquarters, which featured numerous songs penned by the quartet. He also played many of the instruments for the recording as well.

Peter Tork

Born on February 13, 1942 to father Halsten John Thorkelson and mother Virginia Hope in Washington, D.C., Peter Halsten Thorkelson spent most of his childhood days growing up in Washington. He did his schooling from the Windham High School situated in Connecticut and then went on to study at the E.O. Smith High School situated in Connecticut. He then went on to study at the Carleton College. His interest in music was ignited at a very young age and he started taking piano lessons at the age of nine. He knows how to play 12 different instruments which include guitars, banjo, acoustic bass, etc. Before becoming part of the Monkees, Peter shifted to New York City after his college and got himself involved in the folk music in the Greenwich Village. His participation in the band did not only lead to success in the musical fraternity, but as well promoted his acting career.
